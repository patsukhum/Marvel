Marvel Cinematic Universe

Why are Marvel comic book superhero movies so popular?


In April 2019, Marvel’s most recent film, Avengers: Endgame came out, promptly smashing just about every box office record there is to smash (as well as the Avengers facility and much of Upstate New York). Not only did Endgame snag the record of biggest opening weekend in the world at $1.2 billion, but It blew past Avatar’s previous record of total box office revenue, reaching a new record of $2.79 billion in July.

In fact, setting aside some notable exceptions like Avatar, it seems as though almost all the biggest movies these days are action-packed comic book stories adapted for the big screen. Let’s look at some data to see if this is really the case:

Some interesting conclusions can be drawn from the patterns exhibited by the sizes of the circles.

  • Sequels like the Harry Potter series, the Star Wars series, or the Fast and Furious series demonstrate continuous popularity over each release throughout the years.
  • The biggest circles in each genre are mostly movies from these sequels.
  • Apart from the Marvel movies, Avatar and Star Wars: The Force Awakens deserve recognition for their high box office records.
  • DC Extended Universe (DC) movies, while there may be few, exhibit relatively consistent circle sizes, indicating a rather consistent fan base.

Marvel vs. DC Comics

Without a doubt, the most dominant franchises in the comic book movie genre are Marvel and DC. Both the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) and DC Extended Universe (DCEU) have been churning out huge numbers of successful (and not so successful) movies, ever since Iron Man came out in 2008. Both stayed neck-and-neck in terms of yearly box office revenues until the mid 2010’s, after MCU’s Iron Man 2 came out. After that, it’s simply no contest: MCU is the dominant comic book movie franchise (sorry DC fans).

Global Presence of Marvel














One of the defining features (one might say, 'superpowers') of the MCU is the sheer number of characters and stories that have been woven into its single timeline. Beginning with Iron Man in 2008, MCU began producing movies with increasing frequency and increasing success. Now in 2019, there have been a total of 23 MCU movies from 11 different series, with plans for at least 7 more in the near future.

The Marvel Cinematic Universe

This is a timeline of movies as they were released. However, this linear timeline only tells part of the story.

In addition to the branching plots, the relations between each of the many main characters in MCU are highly complex. To get a sense of this, we downloaded the Wikipedia pages of each MCU main character using the Wikimedia API and extracted the links between character pages. Each link represents some sort of relation between the two characters. For example, if Iron Man links to Hulk, then somewhere on Iron Man’s page is a reference to Hulk, meaning that the two characters did something together.

We analyzed this graph to determine who the most "important" character in the MCU was based on Wikipedia links. Drumroll please...

What makes a hero?

It appears that the characters with the largest influence - Captain America, Iron Man, Thor – tend to also have a similar set of powers – super-strength, speed, intelligence – as well as a distinctive weapon and perhaps flight. On the other hand, characters with lower influence tend to be equipped with a different suite of powers and abilities.

Who is the strongest character?

Of course, the relationships between characters are only part of the story. These are superheros after all - what about their powers? We can look at two kinds of data: abilities (quantitative traits such as speed, strength, etc) and powers (specific superpowers such as healing, flight, etc.). Looking down the columns, we can see that super strength, speed, intelligence, and flight are much more common than, say, healing or magical powers. The future of the MCU?

After Endgame, it’s hard to imagine where the MCU might go next. Yet, many more movies are in the works - the so-called “Phase 4” of the MCU - including Black Widow, Spider-Man III, Black Panther II, and many more. With this next set of movies, only one thing is certain: comic book movies and the MCU are not going anywhere.
