In April 2019, Marvel’s final mark of its 11 year series, Avengers: Endgame came out, promptly smashing just about every box office record there is to smash (as well as the Avengers facility and much of Upstate New York). Not only did Endgame snag the record of biggest opening weekend in the world at $1.2 billion, but also did it blow past Avatar’s previous record of total box office revenue, reaching a new record of $2.79 billion in July.

In fact, setting aside some notable exceptions like Avatar, it seems as though almost all the biggest movies these days are action-packed comic book stories adapted for the big screen. Let’s look at some data to see if this is really the case:


Superhero movies vs. Other Genres

Since the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) series started in 2008 (with Iron Man and The Incredible Hulk) and saw its curtains close in 2019 with Avengers: Endgame, we examine the top 100 box office records from each year, from 2008 to 2019. Examining the box office revenue over the years by genre, one thing becomes apparent: comic book movies are truly dominant!

Marvel vs. DC Comics

Without a doubt, Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) and the DC Extended Universe (DCEU) dominate the decade as the most dominant franchises in the comic book genre. While they both have been churning out successful (and not so successful) movies, 2010's Iron Man 2 starts a steep win for MCU. After that, it’s simply no contest: MCU is the dominant comic book movie franchise (sorry DCEU fans).














One of the defining features (one might say, 'superpowers') of the MCU is the sheer number of characters and stories that have been woven into its single timeline. Beginning with Iron Man in 2008, MCU began producing movies with increasing frequency and success. Now in 2019, there have been a total of 23 MCU movies from 11 different series, with plans for at least 7 more in the near future.

Global Presence of Marvel

In addition to the branching plots, the relations between each of the many main characters in MCU are highly complex. To get a sense of this, we downloaded the Wikipedia pages of each MCU main character using the Wikipedia API and extracted the links between character pages. Each link represents some sort of relation between the two characters. For example, if Iron Man links to Hulk, then somewhere on Iron Man’s page is a reference to Hulk, meaning that the two characters did something together.

We analyzed this graph to determine who the most "important" character in the MCU was based on Wikipedia links. Drumroll please...

Superhero Stats

It appears that the characters with the largest influence - Captain America, Iron Man, Thor – tend to also have a similar set of powers – super-strength, speed, intelligence – as well as a distinctive weapon. On the other hand, characters with lower influence tend to be equipped with a different suite of powers and abilities.












The future of the MCU?

After Endgame, it’s hard to imagine where the MCU might go next. Yet, many more movies are in the works - the so-called “Phase 4” of the MCU - including Black Widow, Spider-Man III, Black Panther II, and many more. With this next set of movies, only one thing is certain: comic book movies and the MCU are not going anywhere.
