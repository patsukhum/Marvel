Is the hype real?

In April 2019, Marvel’s final mark of its 11 year series, Avengers: Endgame came out, promptly smashing just about every box office record there is to smash (as well as the Avengers facility and much of Upstate New York). Not only did Endgame snag the record of biggest opening weekend in the world at $1.2 billion, but it also blow past Avatar’s previous record of total box office revenue, reaching a new record of $2.79 billion in July.

In fact, it seems as though almost all the biggest movies these days are action-packed comic book stories adapted for the big screen. Let’s look at some data to see if this is really the case.

Marvel vs. DC Comics: Who's the winner?


Without a doubt, Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) and the DC Extended Universe (DCEU) are the most dominant franchises in the comic book genre. While they both have been churning out successful (and not so successful) movies, 2010's Iron Man 2 starts a steep win for MCU. After that, it’s simply no contest: MCU is the dominant comic book movie franchise (sorry DCEU fans).













What is MCU's secret superpower?

One of the defining features (one might say, 'superpowers') of the MCU is the sheer number of characters and stories that have been woven into its single timeline. Beginning with Iron Man in 2008, MCU began producing movies with increasing frequency and success. Now in 2019, there have been a total of 23 MCU movies from 11 different series, with plans for at least 7 more in the near future.

So How did Marvel take over the world?

From its dense and complex storyline populated with unique characters to hilarious cameos and post-credits appearances, Marvel has truly dominated the movie interesting in the past 11 years. As the world sends off the Infinity Saga and prepares for Phase Four of the Marvel Cinematic Universe, we expect (and hope) this trend to continue!












The future of the MCU?

After Endgame, it’s hard to imagine where the MCU might go next. Yet, many more movies are in the works - the so-called “Phase 4” of the MCU - including Black Widow, Spider-Man III, Black Panther II, and many more. With this next set of movies, only one thing is certain: comic book movies and the MCU are not going anywhere.

Data Sources

All our source codes, data, and images can be found in our GitHub repo.

  • Data: Top 100 box office movie revenues from 2008-2019 were collected from Box Office Mojo. Movie details (Pat) Movie details were collected from MCU Wiki. Wikipedia data on character links were collected using the Python wikipedia package and the MediaWiki API. Eigenvector centrality was computed using the Python networkx package. Matrix data was populated from our movie knowledge as well as from Marvel website. Top 22 characters were selected based on their impact on the overall MCU storyline.
  • Methods: Many pieces of data were found and populated via online resources, where our judgment dictated specific selections. We tried to be as objective as possible.
  • Images: None of the images (except our faces) are ours; the images were collected from Google image search, and respective source links are commented in each image file. Superhero icons were found from Noun Project.
